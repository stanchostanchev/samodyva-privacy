Samodyva — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 7, 2026

The short version: Samodyva collects no data. None. Everything the app does happens entirely on your device. The app does not use the internet at all.

Who we are

Samodyva is a fairy-tale app for children published by Stancho Stanchev. It turns child-style drawings into gentle, original fairy tales using artificial intelligence that runs completely on your device.

What data we collect

None. Samodyva does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information — from children or adults. Specifically, the app has:

  • No user accounts and no sign-in
  • No analytics, telemetry, or crash reporting services
  • No advertising and no ad networks
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data
  • No access to your contacts, location, microphone, or photos

How the app works without data

The artificial intelligence that writes and narrates the fairy tales runs entirely on your phone or tablet. Drawings, generated tales, saved playlists, and settings are stored only in the app's private storage on your device. They never leave it — the app requires no internet connection and functions fully offline.

Sharing initiated by you

If you choose to share a tale or a playlist (for example by email), the content is handed to the app you select through Android's standard share feature. That sharing happens under your control and under the receiving app's privacy policy; Samodyva itself sends nothing anywhere.

Children's privacy

Samodyva is designed for children and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and the GDPR in the simplest possible way: since the app collects no data whatsoever, no personal information from children is ever gathered or processed.

Purchases

Samodyva is a paid app purchased through Google Play. The purchase is processed entirely by Google Play under Google's privacy policy; we receive no personal information about you from it.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app ever changes any of the above, this policy will be updated here before that version is released, and the "last updated" date will change.
